*INTERPOL Notice for Oleg Evgenievich Nefedov*

In an article from *The Hacker News* by Ravie Lakshmanan, it is reported that Ukrainian and German
law enforcement agencies are working together to take down a Russian ransomware-as-a-service group
known as Black Basta. The alleged leader of this group, Oleg Evgenievich Nefedov, has been put on
the EU most wanted list and INTERPOL's Red Notice lists.

The Black Basta group is accused of hacking into protected systems and preparing ransomware attacks.
The group specializes in hash cracking as an infiltration method, and would deploy ransomware once
access has been gained. Dating back to 2022, the group is expected to have earned hundreds of millions
of dollars through their attacks across the globe.

In 2025, there was a leak of internal communications within the Black Basta group. There is a linked
article in the main article explaining what was leaked in greater detail: attack strategies and stolen
credentials from infostealer malware logs. Perhaps the most important information from the leak was
information leading to the pinning of Nefedov as the group's leader, in addition to evidence suggesting
he has direct links to the Russian government. Knowing Nefedov and his aliases ties him into a past
malware group as well. Conti was a group that the U.S. State Department had on its radar in 2020.
There was a 10 million dollar reward for information on a person known as Tramp, which one of
Nefedov's aliases.

While these leaks will likely cause Black Basta to disperse, these hacker groups are known to re-
organize and appear again under a different name. Already, some members of Black Basta are
suspected to have migrated to a group called CACTUS due to the spike in activity attributed to that
group coinciding with the demise of Black Basta.

To tie the information presented in this article to our discussions in class, the MEECES framework can
be applied. Black Basta certainly checks the box for money, and potentially for cause/ideology as well.
The group is not confirmed to be linked to the Russian government, but the advanced capabilites of the
group point to some potential assistance or protection from the government. If true, the government
could then leverage the group to attack targets it deems to be threats.
